Priorities for Foster City

Housing
We are built out! We cannot support additional growth by continuing high density building. It is not sustainable.
Our city faces infrastructure challenges: water, power grid, and traffic to and from our city.
With my vast understanding and knowledge of city zoning.
I will engage with State Legislators and ABAG to educate them on Foster City's infrastructure limitations, and how the mandates do not work in Foster City.

Structural Deficit
Foster City is projecting a financial deficit in the next few years. Our city will need to overcome inflation, rising interest rates to make ends meet.
In the past, Foster City has taken proactive steps for a "soft landing", with reserves. Our Council needs to investigate new revenue opportunities and explore operational efficiencies.

Recreational Amenities
Foster City’s parks are our keystone. I do not support taking away amenities such as our Golf Course and replacing it with housing.
We must protect Foster City Parks while focusing on improving and increasing our community amenities.

Infrastructure
As a Council member I prioritize the repair of our aging infrastructure and successful completion of the Wastewater Treatment Plant.
I will work with the Community to deliver an exciting Recreation Center for us to enjoy for many years to come.

Our Levee
I will fight to get credit for our $90 million investment should the OneShoreline plan move forward to tax all residents in San Mateo County for a county wide levee.

Public Safety
With crime increasing within our City, we must keep our community safe by supporting our police officers and firefighters with essential resources. We must continue to invest in retention, training, and technology.
As your Councilmember, I will propose a Citywide Neighborhood Watch Program. I will publicly encourage participation in our CERT and Community Police Academy programs.
